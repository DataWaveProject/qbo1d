Model description¶

The model is a hybrid of the models ued by Holton and Lindzen (1972) and Plumb (1977). The equation is:

(1)¶\[\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + w \frac{\partial u}{\partial z} - K \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial z^2} = G(z,t) - S(u, z),\]

where \(u\) is the unknown zonal wind, \(t\) and \(z\) are the time and vertical coordinate, \(w\) and \(K\) are constant vertical advection and diffusivity, \(G(z, t)\) is the semi-annual oscilation (see Holton and Lindzen, 1972, defaults to zero in the code), and \(S(u, z)\) is the wave forcing consisting of a sum of monochromatic waves, and parameterizing the momentum deposition at critical levels as follows:

(2)¶\[S(u, z) = \frac{1}{\rho} \frac{\partial}{\partial z} F(u, z),\]

where \(\rho(z)\) is the density profile and the wave flux \(F(u, z)\) is parameterized as follows:

(3)¶\[F(u, z) = \sum_{i} A_{i} \exp\left\{ - \int_{z_1}^{z} g_{i}(u, z') \, dz' \right\},\]

where \(A_{i}\) are the wave amplitudes and

\[g_{i}(u, z) = \frac{\alpha(z) N}{k_{i}(u-c_{i})^2},\]

where \(k_{i}\) and \(c_{i}\) are the wavenumbers and phase speeds, \(N\) is the Brunt-Vaisala frequency taken here to be constant, and \(\alpha(z)\) is the wave dissipation due to infrared cooling (see Holton and Lindzen, 1972).

Note, the sign convention used in the code applies \(-S(u, z)\) on the RHS of the equation, hence the lack of minus sign in the definition of the drag in (2).
